bio

I grew up in India, trained as an Electronics & Telecommunication engineer at Pune University, and took an MBA at IIT Kharagpur. I joined IBM India in 2006 as a SAP Supply Chain consultant and spent the first decade deep in supply chain and ERP-led business transformation across the global steel, metals, and manufacturing industry — enough plants, mills, and refineries to build a strong intuition for how physical businesses actually run.

I moved to IBM Singapore in 2016 and pivoted into analytics, then AI, then GenAI. I've picked up each wave as it landed — SPSS, then Python, then deep learning, then GenAI, and now spec-driven coding — so I can keep bringing innovative solutions to clients that add real value to their business. I am an Associate Partner in IBM Consulting's Singapore practice; my DBA research sits inside the same question: how GenAI is remaking the economics and delivery model of technology consulting.

I am a self-starter, product-minded, and services-oriented. I like working at the boundary between technical teams and business stakeholders where translation is the actual work. I hold two US patents and have written in peer-reviewed venues on the business case for AI, AI ethics, predictive maintenance scheduling, and green IT.
